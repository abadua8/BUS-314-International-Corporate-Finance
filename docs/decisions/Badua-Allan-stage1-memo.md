## Executive Summary

Caterpillar Inc. (NYSE: CAT) is the world's leading manufacturer of construction and mining equipment, with operations spanning more than 190 countries. This memo frames a comprehensive accounting ratio analysis of Caterpillar's recent financial performance. The objective is to move beyond raw financial statement figures and translate them into meaningful, comparable measures across six ratio categories — Performance, Profitability, Efficiency, Leverage, Liquidity, and Du Pont. The analysis will draw on Caterpillar's publicly available 10-K filings and will support a broader assessment of the company's financial health, capital efficiency, and risk profile. The findings will inform internal planning decisions and serve as the foundation for a full Excel model, a technical specification, and a final strategic recommendation in Stages 2 through 4.

---

## Company Background & Objectives

**Company:** Caterpillar Inc. (NYSE: CAT)
**Industry:** Industrial Machinery & Equipment Manufacturing
**Description:** Caterpillar designs, manufactures, and sells construction and mining equipment, diesel and natural gas engines, and industrial gas turbines through its three segments — Construction Industries, Resource Industries, and Energy & Transportation.
**Fiscal Year Under Review:** FY 2022 – FY 2024 (calendar year)

Raw financial statements — the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement — show what happened, but not how well. Ratio analysis normalizes those figures into proportional measures that are comparable across time periods, peer companies, and industry benchmarks. It surfaces insights that dollar figures alone obscure: whether margins are expanding or compressing, whether the company can meet its near-term obligations, how efficiently assets are being deployed, and how much financial risk the capital structure carries. For a company of Caterpillar's scale and cyclicality, these distinctions matter enormously. This analysis will give management a cleaner view of operational momentum and financial resilience heading into a moderating demand cycle.

## Approach

Financial statement data will be sourced from Caterpillar's annual 10-K filings on **SEC EDGAR**, cross-referenced with the **Caterpillar Investor Relations** page (caterpillar.com/investors) and **Yahoo Finance** for market-based inputs. The analysis will cover three fiscal years (FY 2022–2024) to capture trend direction, not just a single-year snapshot.

Six ratio categories will be computed and interpreted:

| Category | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Performance** | Overall earnings power and return relative to revenue and invested capital |
| **Profitability** | Margins at each level of the income statement — gross, operating, and net |
| **Efficiency** | How effectively assets such as inventory and receivables are converted into revenue |
| **Leverage** | The balance between debt and equity financing and the associated financial risk |
| **Liquidity** | Ability to meet short-term obligations without needing to raise additional capital |
| **Du Pont** | Decomposition of return on equity into its margin, asset turnover, and leverage components |

Results will be organized in a structured Excel model, with each ratio category on its own sheet and a summary dashboard for executive review.

## Limitations & Next Steps

**Known constraints:** Caterpillar's consolidated financials include Cat Financial, the company's captive lending arm, which carries significant debt as a normal part of its business model. Leverage ratios may therefore appear elevated relative to pure industrial peers and should be interpreted with that context in mind. Additionally, segment-level margin data requires disaggregation from footnotes, which introduces some manual judgment.

**Next steps by stage:**

- **Stage 2 — Excel Model:** Build a working ratio model from Caterpillar's actual financial data, with clearly labeled formulas, a three-year trend view, and a summary dashboard.
- **Stage 3 — Technical Specification:** Document the model's structure, formula logic, data sources, and key decisions made during construction, along with lessons learned.
- **Stage 4 — Final Analysis Memo:** Deliver a strategic interpretation of the ratio results, identify Caterpillar's financial strengths and risk areas, and provide a clear recommendation — supported by a structured AI prompt used during the analysis.
